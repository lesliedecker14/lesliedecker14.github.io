COLLABORATEUR


Dr. Fabien CIGNETTI is associate professor of Neuroscience at Université Grenoble Alpes since 2017. Prior to this position, he completed postdoctoral training in Neurosciences at Université Aix-Marseille (2012-2017) and in Biomechanics at the University of Nebraska Omaha (2009-2011). He received a PhD in Motor Control at the Université Savoie-Mont Blanc and Università di Trento in 2009.
Dr. Fabien CIGNETTI is broadly interested in the relationship between brain connectivity and motor control in typical and atypical development. Within a cognitive neuroscience framework, his research investigates how improvement in motor control over short (motor learning) to long (motor development) timescales is driven by changes in structural and functional connectivity among brain networks. His research combines multimodal MRI and electroencephalography, psychophysical experiments and computational tools.
Dr. Fabien CIGNETTI serves as a co-PI on the PRESAGE project being in charge of coordinating the MRI work package and serving also as support in the Virtual Reality work package

Ehlers-Danlos syndrome is a mixed group of hereditary diseases whose common feature is a genetic alteration of the connective tissue. The hypermobile type (hEDS) is characterized by generalized joint hypermobility and skin hyperelasticity, which causes somesthesia disorders. The aim of my thesis is to better understand the modulation of muscle synergies underlying locomotor strategies in response to these disorders, and to evaluate the effect of somesthetic perceptual support (i.e., wearing of compressive garments) during sensorimotor reprogramming (i.e., proprioceptive physical therapy) on the modulation of muscle synergies. On a more fundamental level, my work will aim to verify the potential links between the concepts of behavioral complexity (i.e., nonlinear dynamic analysis of kinematic and kinetic fluctuations) and motor control complexity (i.e., number of muscle synergies) during locomotion.
